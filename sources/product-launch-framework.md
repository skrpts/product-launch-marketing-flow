## Product Launch Framework

This framework provides the strategic structure used throughout the product launch marketing flow. It defines launch tiers, planning phases, channel evaluation criteria, and measurement approaches.

### Launch Tier System

#### Tier 1 — Major Launch
**Trigger:** New product, new market entry, rebrand, or company-defining announcement
**Characteristics:**
- Full marketing mobilisation (all channels active)
- Executive involvement in messaging and approval
- PR campaign with media briefings and embargo
- Dedicated landing page and campaign microsite
- Email sequence to full customer and prospect database
- Paid advertising across multiple channels
- Sales enablement package (battle cards, demo scripts, FAQ)
- Customer success preparation (training, documentation, support protocols)
- Post-launch event or webinar

**Timeline:** 6-8 weeks of preparation
**Budget guidance:** Significant portion of quarterly marketing budget

#### Tier 2 — Significant Launch
**Trigger:** Major feature release, new pricing, strategic partnership, or notable product milestone
**Characteristics:**
- Marketing team mobilisation (not full company)
- Blog post and email announcement
- Social media campaign
- Targeted paid advertising
- Sales team briefing
- Updated product pages and documentation
- Optional: media outreach to relevant trade outlets

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks of preparation
**Budget guidance:** Moderate allocation from ongoing marketing budget

#### Tier 3 — Incremental Launch
**Trigger:** Feature update, bug fix that resolves common complaint, minor enhancement
**Characteristics:**
- In-app announcement or changelog update
- Email to affected or interested customer segment
- Social media post
- Updated help documentation
- No paid advertising required

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks of preparation
**Budget guidance:** Minimal, covered by existing content production resources

### The Launch Planning Canvas

Use this canvas to capture launch strategy on a single page:

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Product** | What are we launching? |
| **Why now?** | What makes this timely? |
| **For whom?** | Primary and secondary audiences |
| **Problem solved** | What pain point does this address? |
| **Key benefit** | The single most compelling benefit |
| **Proof** | Evidence that the benefit is real |
| **Competition** | What alternatives exist? Why are we better? |
| **Channels** | How will we reach the audience? |
| **Success metric** | How will we know it worked? |
| **Risk** | What could go wrong? |

### Positioning Framework

The standard positioning template:

> **For** [target audience] **who** [need/problem],
> **[Product]** is a **[category]** that **[key benefit]**.
> **Unlike** [competitive alternative], **[product]** [key differentiator].

**Testing the positioning:**
1. **True?** Can every claim be substantiated with evidence?
2. **Relevant?** Does the target audience actually care about this?
3. **Differentiated?** Would a competitor be unable to make the same claim?
4. **Memorable?** Can someone repeat it after hearing it once?

If the positioning fails any of these tests, revise before proceeding.

### Channel Evaluation Matrix

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Audience reach | 25% | Can this channel reach the target audience at scale? |
| Contextual relevance | 20% | Is the channel contextually appropriate for the message and product? |
| Cost efficiency | 20% | What is the cost per impression, engagement, or conversion? |
| Team capability | 15% | Can the team execute well on this channel within the timeline? |
| Measurement | 10% | Can results be accurately measured and attributed? |
| Speed to impact | 10% | How quickly does this channel generate results? |

Score each channel 1-5 on each criterion. Multiply by weight. Rank by total score.

### Launch Measurement Framework

**The Metric Pyramid:**

```
        [North Star]
       /            \
  [Leading]    [Lagging]
     /    \      /    \
[Diagnostic Metrics]
```

- **North Star:** The single metric that best represents launch success (typically a conversion or adoption metric)
- **Leading indicators:** Metrics visible in the first 48 hours that predict North Star outcome (traffic, sign-up rate, email open rate)
- **Lagging indicators:** Metrics visible after 2-4 weeks that confirm success (retention, revenue, NPS)
- **Diagnostic metrics:** Granular metrics investigated when results are unexpected (page load time, form abandonment, feature adoption)

### Common Launch Failure Modes

1. **Positioning failure:** The messaging does not resonate because it is not differentiated or not relevant
2. **Timing failure:** The launch conflicts with a competitor announcement, holiday, or news event
3. **Channel failure:** Marketing effort is spread across too many channels, executing none well
4. **Coordination failure:** Different teams (marketing, sales, product, support) deliver inconsistent messages
5. **Measurement failure:** No tracking in place, so it is impossible to know what worked
6. **Expectation failure:** Goals were unrealistic given budget, market size, or product readiness
7. **Follow-through failure:** Strong launch week but no sustained marketing, so momentum dies within days
